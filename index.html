HISTORY

Province of Abra Tawi-tawi is an island province in the Philippines located in the Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao. It is known for its rich history and cultural heritage. Tawi-tawi has been inhabited by various ethnic groups, including the Sama-Bajau, Tausug, and Badjao peoples. The history of Tawi-tawi can be traced back to ancient times when it was part of the Sultanate of Sulu. The Sultanate of Sulu was a Muslim state that ruled over parts of present-day Philippines and Malaysia. Tawi-tawi served as an important trading hub for the Sultanate, with its strategic location along the trade routes between Southeast Asia and the Middle East. During the Spanish colonial period, Tawi-tawi was under Spanish rule. However, the local Muslim communities fiercely resisted Spanish colonization, leading to several uprisings and conflicts. The Spanish presence in Tawi-tawi was limited, and the island remained largely autonomous. In the early 20th century, Tawi-tawi became part of the American-controlled Philippines. The island played a significant role during World War II as a base for Japanese forces. After the war, Tawi-tawi was incorporated into the newly independent Republic of the Philippines. Tawi-tawi is known for its beautiful beaches, diverse marine life, and vibrant cultural traditions. It is a popular destination for tourists seeking to explore its natural wonders and learn about its unique heritage.

GEOGRAPHY

Abra is a province located in the Cordillera Administrative Region of the Philippines, known for its stunning mountainous landscapes and rich cultural heritage. It is home to the majestic Mount Pulag, the third-highest peak in the Philippines, attracting hikers and nature enthusiasts from around the world. The province is also famous for its terraced rice fields, particularly in the town of Banaue, which are UNESCO World Heritage Sites and a testament to the ingenuity of the indigenous peoples who have inhabited the region for centuries.

DEMOGRAPHICS

As of the latest available data, Abra has a diverse population primarily composed of indigenous peoples belonging to various ethnic groups such as the Tingguians, Ilocanos, and Kankanaeys. The province has a relatively small population compared to other provinces in the Philippines, with a majority residing in rural areas engaged in agriculture, particularly rice farming. Abra has a youthful population, with a significant portion being under the age of 30, and the province experiences a relatively high birth rate compared to the national average. However, like many rural areas in the Philippines, Abra faces challenges such as outmigration, particularly among young adults seeking better economic opportunities in urban centers or abroad.

ECONOMY

Abra's economy is primarily agrarian, with agriculture serving as the main source of livelihood for a significant portion of the population. Rice farming is the dominant agricultural activity, with other crops such as corn, vegetables, and root crops also cultivated. Livestock raising, particularly swine and poultry farming, is another important aspect of the agricultural sector.
